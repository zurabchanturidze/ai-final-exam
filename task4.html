Ilia State University — GPA Calculation Policy

At Ilia State University, the GPA (Grade Point Average) is the sum, across all courses, of each course's Grade Point (GP) multiplied by its credits, divided by the total credits of the courses the student has passed:

GPA = Σ(GP × credits) / Σ(credits of passed courses)

GP is a course grade converted onto a 4-point scale.

A grade in the E category is always a fixed 1.00 GP. Grades in the D, C, B and A categories (61–95 points) rise in equal steps of 1/12 (≈0.08) per point. A grade in the A+ category is fixed at 4.00 GP.

Within the E and A+ ranges the GP does not change point-to-point, because the university treats every score in those ranges as equally low (E) or equally outstanding (A+).

Additional rules

  1. GPA is issued for every academic program at Ilia State University except doctoral studies; it is not issued for certificate programs.
  2. The university only issues one final, cumulative GPA, shown on the diploma supplement (this does not apply to the single-cycle Medical Doctor program).
  3. The final GPA is rounded to one decimal place.
